Key Areas and Landmarks

1. The Mall: The heart of Wellington Green, the mall itself is a sprawling complex housing a diverse range of retail outlets. From high-street fashion brands to independent boutiques, electronics stores, and department stores, the mall offers a comprehensive shopping experience.

2. Courtenay Place: A vibrant pedestrianized street, Courtenay Place is renowned for its lively atmosphere, bustling restaurants, and eclectic shops. It is a hub for entertainment, with numerous bars, clubs, and live music venues.

3. Cuba Street: Known for its bohemian spirit, Cuba Street is a haven for independent artists, designers, and vintage stores. It is a unique blend of creativity and commerce, offering a diverse range of goods and experiences.

4. The Wellington Botanic Garden: A serene oasis within the city, the Wellington Botanic Garden offers stunning views and a tranquil escape from the urban bustle. It is a popular destination for walks, picnics, and enjoying the beauty of nature.

5. The Museum of New Zealand Te Papa Tongarewa: A world-class museum showcasing New Zealand’s history, art, and culture, Te Papa Tongarewa is a must-visit for anyone interested in the country’s heritage.

6. The Wellington City Library: A modern and welcoming space, the Wellington City Library offers a vast collection of books, resources, and events. It is a hub for learning, creativity, and community engagement.

7. The National Gallery of New Zealand: A renowned art gallery showcasing a diverse collection of New Zealand and international art, the National Gallery offers a unique glimpse into the world of visual arts.

8. The Wellington Waterfront: A picturesque area along the harbour, the Wellington Waterfront is a popular spot for walks, dining, and enjoying the city’s maritime heritage.
