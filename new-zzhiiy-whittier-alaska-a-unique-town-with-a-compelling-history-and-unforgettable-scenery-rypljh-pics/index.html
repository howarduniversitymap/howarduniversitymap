Whittier, Alaska: A Unique Town with a Compelling History and Unforgettable Scenery

Nestled amidst the breathtaking grandeur of the Chugach Mountains and the pristine waters of Prince William Sound, Whittier, Alaska, stands as a testament to human ingenuity and resilience. This small town, with a population of just over 200, boasts a unique history, a captivating landscape, and a captivating story to tell.

A Town Shaped by History and Geography:

Whittier’s story is deeply intertwined with its unique geography. Situated at the head of a narrow fjord, the town’s access is limited, with only a single road connecting it to the outside world. This isolation, however, has played a crucial role in shaping its identity.

During World War II, Whittier served as a strategic military base, known as "Fort Richardson," housing over 7,000 soldiers and playing a vital role in the defense of Alaska. This military presence led to the construction of a vast complex of buildings, including the iconic "Begich Towers," a 14-story building that once housed a large portion of the town’s residents.

After the war, the military presence dwindled, and the town was transferred to the Alaska Railroad. This marked a new chapter in Whittier’s history, with the focus shifting from defense to tourism and fishing. Today, Whittier is a popular destination for visitors seeking adventure, breathtaking scenery, and a glimpse into a unique way of life.

A Single-Building Town:

Whittier’s most striking feature is its remarkable housing situation. The Begich Towers, once a military barracks, now serve as the primary residence for many of the town’s inhabitants. This unique arrangement, with multiple families living within a single building, has become an integral part of Whittier’s identity.

Living in the Begich Towers offers a distinct experience. While it provides a sense of community and shared living, it also presents unique challenges in terms of privacy and space. The building houses apartments, commercial spaces, a community center, and even a post office, making it a self-contained hub within the town.

Exploring Whittier’s Beauty and Adventure:

Whittier offers a range of attractions for visitors, from breathtaking natural landscapes to exciting outdoor activities.

  • The Portage Glacier: A short drive from Whittier, the Portage Glacier offers a mesmerizing sight, with its majestic ice face towering over the water. Visitors can take a boat tour to get up close to the glacier, experiencing its awe-inspiring beauty and learning about its geological significance.

  • Prince William Sound: The surrounding waters of Prince William Sound offer a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Kayaking, whale watching, and fishing are popular activities, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in the pristine beauty of the region and encounter its diverse marine life.

  • Hiking and Trail Exploration: The Chugach Mountains provide ample opportunities for hiking and exploring. From short walks to challenging climbs, the area offers a variety of trails for all skill levels, leading to stunning viewpoints and hidden gems.

  • The Whittier Tunnel: This unique tunnel, spanning over two miles, connects Whittier to the rest of Alaska. It’s a testament to engineering ingenuity and a vital link to the outside world. Visitors can drive through the tunnel, experiencing its unique atmosphere and appreciating its role in the town’s history.

Living in Whittier:

Living in Whittier offers a unique experience, far removed from the hustle and bustle of city life. The town’s isolation fosters a strong sense of community, with residents relying on each other for support and camaraderie.

While Whittier may not be for everyone, it holds a special appeal for those seeking a simpler life, surrounded by nature’s beauty. The town’s tight-knit community, its unique housing arrangements, and its breathtaking surroundings create a captivating and unforgettable living experience.

FAQs about Whittier, Alaska:

Q: How do I get to Whittier?

A: Whittier is accessible by road via the Anton Anderson Memorial Tunnel, which connects the town to the rest of Alaska. The tunnel is approximately 2.5 miles long and is open to traffic 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Q: What is the best time to visit Whittier?

A: The best time to visit Whittier is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is mild and the days are long. However, visitors can experience the town’s unique charm and beauty throughout the year, with each season offering its own distinct appeal.

Q: What are some things to do in Whittier?

A: Whittier offers a range of activities for visitors, including hiking, kayaking, whale watching, fishing, glacier tours, and exploring the town’s history.

Q: What is the cost of living in Whittier?

A: The cost of living in Whittier can be higher than in other parts of Alaska due to its remote location and limited access. However, the town offers a unique and rewarding experience for those seeking a simpler lifestyle.

Q: What is the population of Whittier?

A: The population of Whittier is approximately 200, making it one of the smallest towns in Alaska.

Q: Is Whittier safe?

A: Whittier is a safe town, with a low crime rate. However, it is important to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings, especially when exploring remote areas.
